Why Laurel Homes Need Radon Mitigation

Laurel sits in the Yellowstone River valley within Yellowstone County, an area designated as EPA Zone 1 for radon risk. The geological makeup of the region, featuring layers of sedimentary rock including sandstone, shale, and limestone, creates natural pathways for radon gas to rise from the earth and enter homes through foundation cracks, gaps around pipes, and other openings. Many homes in Laurel have basements, which are particularly vulnerable to radon intrusion because they are in direct contact with the surrounding soil.

One of the challenges specific to Laurel is the age of much of its housing stock. Homes built in the mid-twentieth century during Laurel's growth as a railroad town typically lack any radon-resistant construction features. These older foundations are more likely to have cracks, settling, and gaps that allow radon gas to enter freely. Even newer homes in Laurel can have elevated radon levels, as the underlying geology is the primary driver of radon risk regardless of when a home was built.

Montana's harsh winters compound the problem. When Laurel homeowners seal their homes against the cold, they also trap radon gas inside, allowing it to accumulate to potentially dangerous levels. The stack effect created by heating a home during winter can actually draw radon-laden soil gas into the basement through negative pressure differentials. Testing is the only way to know if your Laurel home has a radon problem. If levels exceed 4.0 pCi/L, a professionally installed mitigation system can reduce concentrations by up to 99%.
