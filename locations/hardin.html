Radon Mitigation Services in Hardin, Montana

Hardin is the county seat of Big Horn County, located approximately 45 minutes east of Billings along Interstate 90. Situated near the confluence of the Bighorn and Little Bighorn rivers, Hardin is a historic community with deep roots in Montana's agricultural and cultural heritage. The town serves as a gateway to the Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument and is home to a resilient community of families, ranchers, and small business owners who take pride in their southeastern Montana way of life.

Like much of south-central and eastern Montana, the geology beneath Big Horn County produces naturally occurring radon gas. Uranium-bearing soils and sedimentary rock formations release radon as a byproduct of radioactive decay, and this gas can migrate upward through the ground and accumulate inside homes. The EPA recognizes this region as having significant radon potential, and independent testing data confirms that many homes in and around Hardin test above the 4 pCi/L action level. Because radon is completely invisible and odorless, the only way to know your home's radon concentration is through professional testing.

Radon Concerns for Hardin and Big Horn County

Hardin's housing stock includes many older homes built during the town's growth as a regional agricultural hub. These properties often feature basements with aging foundations that have developed cracks, gaps, and settling over decades of Montana weather cycles. Stone, block, and poured-concrete foundations all provide potential pathways for radon entry, particularly when hairline cracks widen due to freeze-thaw cycles and soil movement. Homes located near the river bottoms may sit on alluvial soils that are especially permeable to radon gas migration.

Newer homes in the Hardin area are not immune to radon either. Without specific radon-resistant construction features, even recently built homes can accumulate dangerous concentrations. Montana's cold winters compound the problem by sealing homes tightly against outside air, trapping radon indoors and allowing it to build to levels that significantly increase the risk of lung cancer over time. The EPA estimates that radon is responsible for approximately 21,000 lung cancer deaths annually in the United States, making it the second leading cause after smoking.

Bringing Professional Radon Services to Hardin

Rim Rock Radon extends its licensed and insured services to Hardin and the greater Big Horn County area. Because smaller communities like Hardin may not have local radon mitigation specialists, we make it a priority to serve homeowners throughout the region with the same professional-grade equipment, EPA-approved methods, and performance guarantees that we offer in the Billings metro area.

Our process begins with professional radon testing to establish your home's baseline concentration. If results indicate levels at or above the EPA action level of 4 pCi/L, we design a customized mitigation system based on your home's foundation type and specific radon entry points. The most common solution is active sub-slab depressurization, which creates negative pressure beneath the foundation to intercept radon gas before it enters living spaces. A sealed vent pipe routes the gas to a continuously running fan that exhausts it safely above the roofline.

Every Hardin installation includes post-mitigation testing to verify that radon levels have been reduced to safe concentrations. We provide a written performance guarantee with every system.
