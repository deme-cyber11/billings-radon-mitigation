Radon Testing and Mitigation in Park City, Montana

Park City is a small community of roughly 1,000 residents located about 25 miles southwest of Billings along Interstate 90 in Stillwater County, Montana. Nestled in the Yellowstone River valley with views of the Beartooth Mountains to the south, Park City offers a rural lifestyle with easy access to the services and employment opportunities of the Billings metro area. The town draws families and retirees who appreciate its quiet neighborhoods, affordable housing, and strong sense of community. Homes in Park City range from established properties built in the mid-twentieth century to newer construction on the outskirts of town, and most sit on foundations that are in direct contact with radon-producing soils.

Stillwater County and the surrounding south-central Montana region sit on geological formations that are known to produce elevated levels of naturally occurring radon gas. Uranium deposits in the sedimentary bedrock and glacial soils undergo radioactive decay, generating radon that seeps upward through the ground and enters buildings through cracks in foundations, gaps around pipes, sump openings, and construction joints. The EPA classifies this part of Montana as a Zone 1 area for radon potential, meaning the predicted average indoor radon level exceeds 4 pCi/L. For Park City homeowners, this designation makes professional radon testing not just a recommendation but a necessity for protecting the health of everyone in the household.

Why Radon Is a Serious Concern in Stillwater County

Radon is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States, responsible for an estimated 21,000 deaths each year according to the EPA. Because the gas is invisible, odorless, and tasteless, it can accumulate to dangerous concentrations inside a home without any warning signs. In Stillwater County, the combination of uranium-rich geology, cold winters that keep homes sealed tight for months, and the prevalence of basements and below-grade living spaces creates conditions that are particularly favorable for indoor radon buildup. Many Park City homes were built during periods when radon-resistant construction techniques were not yet standard practice, leaving older foundations especially vulnerable to gas infiltration through deteriorating mortar joints, floor-wall seams, and settlement cracks.

Well water is another consideration for Park City residents. Homes that rely on private wells drilled into uranium-bearing rock formations may have dissolved radon in their water supply. When this water is used indoors for showers, dishwashing, and laundry, radon is released into the air and adds to the overall indoor concentration. While airborne radon from soil gas is typically the primary source of exposure, waterborne radon can contribute meaningfully in areas with the right geological conditions. Our team evaluates both pathways when assessing radon risk for Park City homes, ensuring that mitigation strategies address the complete picture rather than just a single entry point.

Professional Radon Mitigation for Park City Homes

Rim Rock Radon serves Park City and all of Stillwater County with licensed and insured radon reduction services. Our process begins with a professional radon test using calibrated continuous monitoring equipment to establish accurate baseline readings in your home. If test results show radon concentrations at or above the EPA action level of 4 pCi/L, we design a custom mitigation system engineered specifically for your home's foundation type, soil characteristics, and construction details. The most common and effective solution is an active sub-slab depressurization system, which creates negative pressure beneath the foundation slab to intercept radon gas before it can enter living spaces. A sealed PVC vent pipe carries the gas to a quiet, energy-efficient fan that exhausts it safely above the roofline where it disperses harmlessly into the outdoor air.

For Park City homes with crawl spaces, we install sub-membrane depressurization systems that seal the exposed soil with a heavy-duty polyethylene vapor barrier and draw radon from beneath it using the same active suction principle. Homes with complex foundations that combine slab sections with crawl spaces may require a hybrid approach, and our technicians have the experience to engineer solutions for any configuration. Every installation is completed with minimal disruption to your home, typically in a single day, and includes post-mitigation testing to verify that radon levels have been reduced to safe concentrations. We back all of our work with a performance guarantee: if your levels are not brought below 4 pCi/L, we return and make adjustments at no additional cost.
