Why Shepherd Homes Need Radon Mitigation

Shepherd's location in northern Yellowstone County places it within one of Montana's highest radon risk zones. The area's terrain, which transitions from the Yellowstone River valley to the rolling hills and benchlands north of Billings, is underlain by sedimentary formations that are natural sources of uranium and radium. As these elements decay, they produce radon gas that migrates upward through the soil and can enter homes through any opening in the foundation, including cracks, construction joints, gaps around utility penetrations, and even through porous concrete.

Radon levels in the Shepherd area can vary significantly from one property to the next, even among neighboring homes. This variability is due to differences in the underlying soil composition, foundation construction, and how tightly each home is sealed. A home just down the road from yours might have perfectly safe radon levels while your home tests dangerously high. The only way to know your home's radon level is through professional testing. The EPA and the Montana Department of Environmental Quality both recommend that every home be tested for radon, regardless of location or age.

Rural homes in the Shepherd area often have features that can increase radon risk, including well water systems that may contain dissolved radon, larger basement footprints, and homes built directly on soil without the benefit of municipal infrastructure. Our team has extensive experience mitigating radon in rural residential properties and will design a system that accounts for your home's specific construction and site conditions.
