Columbus is the county seat of Stillwater County, a small town of roughly 2,000 residents nestled along the Yellowstone River about 40 miles west of Billings on Interstate 90. Known as the gateway to the Beartooth Highway, Columbus blends rural Montana charm with a tight-knit community atmosphere. However, the same geological conditions that shape the Stillwater and Yellowstone River valleys also produce elevated levels of radon gas beneath local homes. Uranium-bearing rock formations deep underground release radon as they decay, and this odorless, colorless gas migrates upward through the soil and into residential foundations. Many Columbus homes feature full basements and lower-level living spaces that sit in direct contact with radon-laden soil, placing families at risk of long-term exposure to the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States.

Why Columbus Homes Need Radon Testing

Columbus sits in the Stillwater Valley, where thousands of years of alluvial deposits from the Yellowstone and Stillwater Rivers have created soil layers that can harbor elevated concentrations of naturally occurring radioactive materials. The EPA classifies Stillwater County as a region with significant radon potential, and many homes in the area have tested above the 4.0 pCi/L action level. Older homes in Columbus, built long before radon-resistant construction techniques became standard, are especially vulnerable. Foundation cracks, gaps around utility penetrations, deteriorating mortar joints, and exposed sump pits all provide easy pathways for radon gas to enter the home. Even newer construction can develop radon problems if passive prevention features were not incorporated during building. The only way to know whether your Columbus home has a radon problem is to test. If elevated levels are found, a professionally installed mitigation system can reduce radon concentrations by up to 99%.
