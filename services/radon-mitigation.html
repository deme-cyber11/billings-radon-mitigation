How Active Soil Depressurization Works

The most effective and widely used radon mitigation method is active soil depressurization (ASD). This system works by creating a vacuum beneath your home's foundation slab, drawing radon-laden soil gas from underneath the house and venting it safely above the roofline before it can enter your living space. A small, continuously running inline fan creates the negative pressure that prevents radon from seeping through cracks, joints, and porous concrete into your home.

The installation process begins with coring one or more suction points through your basement slab. Schedule 40 PVC piping connects these suction points to the inline radon fan, which is typically mounted in the attic or on an exterior wall. The exhaust pipe extends above the roofline, where radon gas disperses harmlessly into the outdoor air. All slab penetrations, cracks, and gaps are sealed with polyurethane caulk to maximize the system's efficiency and performance.

How Radon Mitigation Works in a Billings-Area Home

Sub-slab depressurization is the EPA-recommended method for reducing radon in most homes, and it's the approach Rim Rock Radon uses for the vast majority of Billings-area installations. The process begins with a careful assessment of your home's foundation type, construction, and radon entry points. This diagnostic step is essential — a properly designed system depends on understanding the specific conditions beneath your foundation.

Once we've assessed your home, we drill a small suction point through the concrete floor at a strategic location. A PVC pipe runs from that suction point up through the home and exhausts above the roofline, where a continuously running fan draws radon-laden soil gas up and out before it can enter your living space. The system runs 24/7, requires minimal maintenance, and typically uses less electricity than a light bulb.

After installation, we seal any visible radon entry points — floor cracks, pipe penetrations, sump pits — to maximize system efficiency. Post-mitigation testing 24 to 48 hours after installation confirms that radon levels have dropped to safe levels.
