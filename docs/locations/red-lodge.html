Radon Mitigation Services in Red Lodge, Montana

Red Lodge is a charming mountain town nestled at the base of the Beartooth Mountains in Carbon County, Montana. Known as the gateway to the Beartooth Highway and home to the Red Lodge Mountain ski resort, this community of approximately 2,300 residents attracts visitors year-round for skiing, hiking, fishing, and its vibrant downtown arts and dining scene. Red Lodge's historic architecture, mountain setting, and tourism-driven economy make it one of the most distinctive communities in south-central Montana.

The geological setting around Red Lodge is complex, with mountain soils, glacial deposits, and mineralized rock formations that can produce significant amounts of radon gas. Carbon County is classified by the EPA as having elevated radon potential, and homes throughout the Red Lodge area should be tested regardless of their age or construction type. The mountain valley geography can actually concentrate radon in certain locations, as cold air drainage patterns and tight building envelopes during winter months prevent natural ventilation from diluting indoor radon levels.

Radon Risks for Red Lodge's Older Buildings and Mountain Homes

Red Lodge has a rich architectural heritage, with many buildings in the downtown core and surrounding residential areas dating back to the early 1900s. These older structures were built long before anyone understood the risks of indoor radon, and their foundations often contain numerous pathways for gas entry: cracked mortar joints, settling gaps, unsealed utility penetrations, and deteriorating foundation walls. Stone and brick foundations, common in historic Red Lodge properties, are particularly porous and can allow radon to seep through the walls themselves.

Mountain homes and vacation properties in the Red Lodge area face their own unique radon challenges. Homes built on hillsides may have walk-out basements or lower levels that are partially below grade, creating significant contact area with radon-producing soil. The tight construction needed to withstand Montana mountain winters means these homes are well-sealed, which is excellent for energy efficiency but can trap radon gas indoors. Properties that sit vacant for portions of the year, such as seasonal vacation homes, may accumulate very high radon concentrations during periods of minimal ventilation.

Serving Red Lodge and Carbon County

Billings Radon Mitigation provides licensed and insured radon services to Red Lodge homeowners and property managers throughout Carbon County. Although Red Lodge is located about 60 miles southwest of Billings, we serve the community regularly and maintain the same standards of professionalism and workmanship that our Yellowstone County clients expect.

Our mitigation approach is tailored to the specific conditions found in Red Lodge homes. For most properties, we install active sub-slab depressurization systems that draw radon from beneath the foundation and vent it safely above the roofline. For older buildings with stone or block foundations, we may recommend additional sealing work combined with active depressurization to achieve the best results. Homes with crawl spaces receive sub-membrane depressurization systems that seal the soil surface and extract radon before it can enter the living space.

Every Red Lodge installation includes post-mitigation testing to verify that radon levels have dropped below the EPA action level of 4 pCi/L. We back all our systems with a written performance guarantee.
