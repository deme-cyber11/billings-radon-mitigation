Why Billings Homes Need Radon Mitigation

Billings sits atop geological formations that make it particularly susceptible to elevated radon gas levels. The Yellowstone River valley, combined with the sandstone and shale bedrock common throughout Yellowstone County, creates ideal conditions for radon to accumulate in the soil and seep into residential foundations. According to EPA data, Yellowstone County is classified as a Zone 1 area, meaning it has a predicted average indoor radon screening level greater than 4 pCi/L, the threshold at which the EPA recommends taking corrective action.

Many Billings homes, especially those built before modern radon-resistant construction standards were adopted, have basements and crawl spaces that are directly exposed to radon-laden soil gas. The city's cold winters mean homes are sealed tightly for months at a time, which can cause radon to build up to dangerous concentrations indoors. Studies have shown that a significant percentage of homes tested in the Billings area exceed the EPA action level, with some homes registering radon levels well above 10 pCi/L.

Radon is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States, responsible for an estimated 21,000 deaths annually. The only way to know if your Billings home has a radon problem is to test. If elevated levels are found, a professionally installed mitigation system can reduce radon by up to 99%, making your home safe for your family. Do not wait to find out if your home is at risk.
