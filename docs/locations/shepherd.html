Radon Testing and Mitigation in Shepherd, Montana

Shepherd is a rural community situated northwest of Billings in Yellowstone County, Montana. Known for its agricultural heritage and wide-open spaces, Shepherd offers residents a quieter lifestyle with convenient access to the Billings metro area. The community is home to families who value the rural Montana way of life, with properties that range from traditional farmhouses and ranch-style homes to newer residential builds on larger lots.

Despite its peaceful setting, Shepherd faces the same radon risks as the rest of Yellowstone County. The underlying geology throughout this region is rich in uranium-bearing formations that produce radon gas as a natural byproduct of radioactive decay. This gas migrates through soil and rock, entering homes through any available pathway in the foundation. Because radon is colorless, odorless, and tasteless, the only way to know if your Shepherd home has elevated levels is through professional testing.

Radon Concerns for Rural and Well Water Homes

Homes in the Shepherd area often sit on larger lots with deeper foundations and may rely on well water rather than municipal supplies. While radon in water is generally a smaller concern than radon in soil gas, homes that draw from private wells in uranium-rich geology can have elevated radon concentrations in their water supply. This radon is released into indoor air during showers, laundry, and other water use. Our team can help you understand the full picture of radon exposure in your Shepherd home, including both soil gas and waterborne pathways.

Many Shepherd properties feature basements and crawl spaces that are particularly vulnerable to radon accumulation. Agricultural properties may have older foundations with stone or block construction that has developed cracks and gaps over decades of use. These openings provide direct pathways for radon gas to enter living spaces. Even homes with poured-concrete foundations can develop hairline cracks over time due to soil settlement, freeze-thaw cycles, and the natural aging process.

Our Mitigation Process for Shepherd Homes

Our licensed and insured technicians travel to Shepherd regularly to serve homeowners throughout the community. We begin every project with a professional radon test to establish baseline concentrations. If results show levels at or above the EPA action level of 4 pCi/L, we design a customized mitigation system based on your home's specific foundation type, soil conditions, and radon entry points.

The most common solution is an active sub-slab depressurization system, which creates negative pressure beneath the foundation to intercept radon gas before it enters the home. A sealed vent pipe routes the gas to a fan that exhausts it safely above the roofline, where it dissipates harmlessly into the outdoor air. For homes with crawl spaces, we may recommend sub-membrane depressurization, which involves sealing the crawl space floor with a heavy-duty vapor barrier and drawing radon from beneath it.

Every installation includes post-mitigation testing to confirm that radon levels have been reduced below 4 pCi/L.
