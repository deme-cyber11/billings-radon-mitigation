Why Lockwood Homes Need Radon Mitigation

Lockwood's location in Yellowstone County places it squarely within an EPA Zone 1 radon risk area, where average indoor radon levels are predicted to exceed the 4.0 pCi/L action level. The area's geology is characterized by alluvial deposits from the Yellowstone River and underlying sedimentary bedrock that is naturally rich in uranium, the element that decays to produce radon gas. These geological conditions make radon intrusion a persistent concern for every home in the Lockwood area, regardless of age or construction quality.

Many homeowners in Lockwood may assume that because their homes are relatively new, they are safe from radon. This is a common misconception. While modern building codes in Montana encourage radon-resistant new construction techniques, these passive systems do not always prevent radon from entering a home. The effectiveness of passive radon barriers depends heavily on installation quality and the specific soil conditions beneath the home. Even homes built with radon-resistant features should be tested, as the EPA recommends testing every home regardless of age or construction type.

Lockwood's rapid development means that many homes were built on land that was previously agricultural or undeveloped. Disturbing the soil during construction can create new pathways for radon gas to migrate upward. Combined with Yellowstone County's naturally high radon potential, this makes testing and mitigation essential for Lockwood homeowners who want to protect their families from this invisible, odorless, cancer-causing gas.
