Complete Radon System Installation for Billings Homes

A professionally installed radon mitigation system is the most reliable way to reduce dangerous radon gas levels in your home. In Billings, MT, where elevated radon is common due to the geological characteristics of the Yellowstone River valley, a properly designed and installed system can reduce radon levels by up to 99%. Our team installs active soil depressurization systems that are built to last and engineered for the specific conditions of your property.

Every home is different, which is why we take a custom approach to every installation. Factors like your foundation type, the size of your home, soil permeability beneath the slab, and the layout of your ductwork and utilities all influence how we design your system. Our goal is to achieve maximum radon reduction with minimal visual impact and disruption to your daily life.

System Components: What Gets Installed

A complete radon mitigation system consists of several key components working together. The system begins with a suction point cored through your basement slab or crawl space membrane, creating the entry point where radon-laden air is drawn from the soil. Connected to the suction point is schedule 40 PVC piping that runs from beneath the foundation up through your home and out above the roofline.

A dedicated inline radon fan mounted in the attic or on the exterior wall creates continuous negative pressure beneath the slab, drawing radon from the soil and exhausting it safely above the roof where it dissipates harmlessly into the outdoor air. An exhaust point at the top of the piping ensures the radon is released well above the living space. All cracks, joints, and penetrations in the slab are sealed with specialized polyurethane caulk to maximize system efficiency. A U-tube manometer is installed on the piping so you can visually confirm at a glance that the system is operating properly.

Custom Design for Every Foundation Type

Billings homes feature a variety of foundation types, and each requires a different approach to radon system installation. Slab-on-grade foundations are the most common in newer Billings subdivisions and are ideal for standard sub-slab depressurization systems. Basement foundations, prevalent in older Billings neighborhoods, may require multiple suction points depending on the size and layout of the basement. Crawl space foundations use a sealed membrane approach combined with sub-membrane depressurization to capture radon before it enters the home.

For homes with combination foundations, such as a partial basement with a slab-on-grade addition, our team designs integrated systems that address radon entry from every section of the foundation. We perform diagnostic testing during installation to verify that the suction field extends adequately beneath the entire footprint of your home.

The Installation Process: What to Expect

Our installation process begins with a thorough assessment of your home's foundation type, size, and construction. We determine the optimal number and placement of suction points, plan the piping route to minimize visual impact, and select the appropriate fan size for your property. Most installations are completed in four to six hours. Our crew takes care to protect your home during installation, covering floors and surfaces and cleaning up thoroughly when the job is done.

After installation, we perform a post-mitigation radon test to verify the system is reducing radon levels as expected. We also walk you through the system components, show you how to read the manometer, and provide guidance on ongoing maintenance. Every system we install comes with a performance guarantee and warranty on components, giving you confidence that your home is protected for years to come.
